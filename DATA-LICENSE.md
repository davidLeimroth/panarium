# Data license

This repository is two things at once: source code and a curated corpus of bread
data distilled from third-party sources. Those two parts are licensed
separately, and this page explains how the data side works in plain language.

## Recipe facts are not copyrightable

Quantities, hydration percentages, fermentation times, baking temperatures,
dough yields and similar measurements are statements of fact. Lists of
ingredients and the underlying math of a formula are facts too. No one can own a
fact, and facts in this repository are free for anyone to reuse.

The recipe facts in this repository (the numbers in `formula`, `ingredients`,
`times`, `yield`, and any other measurement fields) are offered to the public
under the terms of [Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/)
(CC BY 4.0).

## The editorial writing is original work

The method summaries, recipe descriptions, keeping notes and origin notes in
this repository are original editorial writing produced for this project. They
are not copied from the sources they cite. They paraphrase and summarise the
underlying technique in the project's own voice.

This original editorial writing is offered under the same terms, CC BY 4.0. If
you reuse it, you must credit the project and link back to
`https://github.com/davidLeimroth/bread-wt-improvements`.

## Keep the source attribution

Every entry in the corpus carries a `source.url` field that points to the
third-party recipe or reference the entry was distilled from. That link is the
attribution chain for the facts.

If you copy, republish or adapt entries from this atlas, you must keep the
`source.url` field intact so that downstream readers can trace each bread back
to the original author. Dropping that field breaks the attribution the CC BY
4.0 license requires, and it is the one thing the license is not permissive
about.

## This is an index, not a replacement

Panarium is an atlas: a structured index that points outward. Every entry is a
distillation of a source recipe, with a link to the original. The goal is to
make bread knowledge searchable and comparable, and to send readers back to the
bakers, cookbooks and writers who did the original work.

Nothing here is meant to substitute for the original recipes. To cook any of
these breads in full, follow the `source.url`.

## Summary

- Source code: see [LICENSE](LICENSE) (MIT).
- Recipe facts (quantities, times, temperatures, ingredient lists): public

- Method summaries, descriptions and other original editorial writing in the
  corpus: CC BY 4.0, credit the project.
- Every entry keeps its `source.url`; reusers must keep that attribution.
